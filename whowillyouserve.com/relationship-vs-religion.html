Relationship vs Religion & Rules

Man's way to God is through religion and rules, neither of which are accepted with Him. In ancient times, God had defined a way for men to come to Him through rules. The children of Israel entered into a covenant with God to keep his statutes and laws.

The book of Hebrews tells us that now there is a better way to access God and find favor in his sight. By a NEW COVENANT that he made through his son Jesus. The old way (covenant) has been voided. God does not want people to be friends with Him by following a defined set of rules. The old way of living
by rules and regulations is GONE!

Or at least it's supposed to be. It can still be found in Amish and various other plain groups living by the "ordnung" to gain favor in God's sight, Jews trying to keep the law, Catholics, and many mainline "protestant" religious churches.

Heck, just about every religious denomination has a predefined set of rules that you have to follow to be a "Christian or member in good standing."

Every hear of these things?
  • Specific style of dress/uniform
  • Can only drive specific types of vehicles
  • Can't eat pork....prohibited from eating/drinking various foods
  • Confess your sins to a man
  • Access to God/favor only through a specific "church" or institution 
  • Pay your tithes and attend church faithfully
  • Be a 5 star Christian  

These are all attempts to reform the outside. To make you look and act like a proper religious member of your organization. Its funny though that this is the exact opposite of the ways of God. Man tries to work from the outside-inward,
but God works from the inside-outward!



God's Book says:
  • the Lord sees not as man sees; for man looks on the outward appearance, but the Lord looks on the heart.
  • For it is God which works in you both to will and to do of his good pleasure.

The Pharisees were a group of Jewish people who lived in ancient Israel during Bible times. They were noted for strict observance of rites and ceremonies of the written law. Their religious sect was brimming full of rules and regulations.

Jesus came on the religious scene and promptly pronounces "Woe" to the pharisees. He thoroughly condemned the established religion of that day! (This is recorded in Mathew 23)

Listen to what he said to them:

  • Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! for ye make clean the outside of the cup and of the platter, but within they are full of extortion and excess.
  • Thou blind Pharisee, cleanse first that which is within the cup and platter, that the outside of them may be clean also.
Crazy stuff, huh?
Jesus came to earth and forged a trail that was completely different than the mainstream religion. He brought in a true way where one could have access and favor with the Supreme Being -Jehovah God. That way was only through himself.

Its still the same today. All around you have religion and different sects that claim to be "the way." You have to make the same choice that people of Jesus' day had to make:

What trail are you going to walk down?
Who will you serve?
Will you have a relationship with Jesus?
Or will you follow man's dead end road of religion and rules.
